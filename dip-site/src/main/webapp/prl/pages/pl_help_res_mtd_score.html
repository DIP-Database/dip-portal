If a given link was inferred by several methods, the value displayed in this column is the score of the method that showed the most significant ProLinks score. For more datails on how method scores are calculated check our main publication (2004, Genome Biol. 5(5):R35).

The table below presents a few guidelines (suggested cutoffs below which inferences can be considered significant) to help you understand the relevance of an inference depending on both the reported score and the method(s) responsible for producing the inference:


Inference method Suggested maximum score
Phylogenetic Profiles -10
Gene Neighbor 1e-20
Gene Cluster 0.01
Rosetta Stone 0.01

Note: Inferences with scores greater than the suggested thresholds can be useful depending on your particular biological question, but they normally correspond to low ProLinks scores (e.g. <0.4).
